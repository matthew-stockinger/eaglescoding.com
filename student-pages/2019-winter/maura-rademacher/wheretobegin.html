Where to Begin

What is Right For You?

When it comes to actually making your ideas a reality, I stick to two different ways of composing: notation and digital production.

Pros and Cons of Notation

If you are starting out with writing music, I suggest that you start with notation before you move on to the more difficult stuff. However, some people like to stick this way of writing music, and it definitely has its pros and cons.

    Pros
  • Easier to use
  • Easier to learn
  • Makes more sense if you want your music to be played live
    Cons
  • Limited tools
  • Can be glitchy
  • Sound quality isn't usually the greatest

There's a good number of notation softwares that I think are really great, my favorites being Noteflight, ScoreCloud, and MuseScore. You can find the links to their websites and/or download links below

  • Noteflight
  • ScoreCloud
  • MuseScore

Pros and Cons of Digital Production

Digital production has recently become my new favorite thing. I've been able to create more realistic sounding music without the hassle of testing my ideas on a piano. That said, it still has its pros and cons.

    Pros
  • Realistic sounds
  • Much more resources
  • Can create a wide variety of genres
    Cons
  • Very complicated to use
  • Built in plugins can suck up a lot of CPU
  • EXPENSIVE!! Most digital audio workstations(DAWs) range in price from 99$ to well over 1000$

Since these programs like to drain your wallet, you should always try the trial version of each DAW you're interested before you buy. I tried out a few DAWs before I settled on the one I thought was the most user friendly, which is Image-Line's FL Studio. Some other popular DAWs are Ableton Live, Avid Pro Tools, and Logic Pro. Download links and websites are below
