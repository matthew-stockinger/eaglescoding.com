Where to Learn

Learning Music Theory

Learning every tiny detail of music theory sucks. There's so many rules on what you can and can't do. I remember watching youtube tutorial after youtube tutorial to try and understand it, and after searching for what felt like years, I have found the best free resource to learning music theory online.

Youtuber MusicTechHelpGuy created in my opinion, the best online series for music theory. He explains everything amazingly and teaches in a way that makes sense, and his 24 part series goes into depth explaining the details of music theory. I believe that it's the best way to learn, because it's free! But all jokes aside, MusicTechHelpGuy is super good at this stuff, so please stop by his channel and subscribe, the dude is super good at what he does.

Now You Know How to Write Music... Now What?

Well now you just have to write it out. There's about 20 million different ways(not really) to do it, but I would just stick to one or two ways to create your music. When I first started, I was writing my ideas into notation, but I've been experimenting with digitally produced music genres such as glitch hop, retrowave, and mid-tempo/dubstep.
