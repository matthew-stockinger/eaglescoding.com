My Projects

Notation and Sheet Music

The first place anyone should go to to create music would be musical notaion. In my opinion, it's the best way to learn how the basics to music theory work. It's also a good way to learn all the different clefs that instuments play in, and it's quite fun to mess with all the keys you can play in.

Finding what inspires you the most

About a few years ago, I became really interested in electonic music. One artist in particular caught my attention. When the artist Karma Fields first released his song "Build The Cities" on the record label Monstercat, I immediately fell in love. Later, that same artist recreated some of his songs into classial style pieces, so I obviously had to make my own arrangement of one of the songs. I arranged the classical version of his song titled "Stickup", which I wrote to be played by an orchestra.
