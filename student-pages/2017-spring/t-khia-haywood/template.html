Sea of Stars

This beautiful sea is in the Maldives. Thia natural phemenon was captures by Taiwanese photographer Will ho, who posted picturs of what he thought was 'blue sand' to Flicker.

This beautiful glowing blue waters are caused by a natural chemical reation known as bioluminescence where mircroorganisms in the water are disturbed by oxygen. The result is a glittering and sparkling sea water, known as the sea of stars. As the waves crash on the beach, the water glows in the dark and acts like a mirror that raflects the sparkling stars above.

The tiny organisms emit light when stressed, be it by the lapping of waves, the carving action of a surf board or other, creating what looks like a network of stars.
